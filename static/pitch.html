Why it wins for live sports
  • True streaming, not chunking. A cache-aware streaming transducer emits each word the moment it's recognized — a constant per-word latency bound (~250 ms typical, <450 ms worst case), not a variable 2–5 s chunk delay. TV captioning norms are 2–5 seconds; we operate an order of magnitude inside that.
  • Built for noisy broadcast audio. Crowd noise, stadium PA, fast commentary — handled by noise-robust acoustic models plus an optional speech-band filter front-end.
  • Words never retract. Committed text is final — safe to push straight to the broadcast graphics pipeline. A live preview of the in-progress word is available separately.
  • Drop-in C++ integration. The engine is a native C++ library. Your application feeds 80 ms PCM chunks and receives committed words — in-process, zero network hops.
  • Selectable accuracy/latency profiles. Lowest-latency (~200 ms), balanced (punctuated, ~300 ms), or maximum-accuracy (~1 s) — switchable per production.
